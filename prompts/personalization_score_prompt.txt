You are an expert in computer science education and personalized curriculum design. 
Your task is to evaluate a generated learning plan based on its *Personalization* for
a specific learner.

CONTEXT:
Learner Profile: {learner_profile_json}
Learning Query: {query_text}
Generated Plan: {generated_plan_json}

EVALUATION INSTRUCTIONS:
Please rate the plan's Personalization on a scale of 0-1 based on the following criteria. 
Provide a step-by-step analysis before giving a final score.

1. Skill & Experience Alignment (0-1): 
Does the plan begin from the learner’s likely knowledge boundary, instead of assuming too much or reteaching too much?
Are the subtasks and steps pitched at an appropriate level of detail and difficulty for this learner?
- A high score means the plan starts where this learner’s understanding is likely to end.
- A low score means the plan is clearly too advanced, too basic, or generic.
- A high score means the plan’s decomposition matches the learner’s level.
- A low score means the plan is too coarse, too fine-grained, or mismatched in difficulty.

2. Goal Effectiveness (0-1): 
How directly does the plan's structure and content contribute
to achieving the learner's actual learning objective implied by the query?
- A high score means the plan’s subtasks and steps consistently support meaningful progress toward understanding and solving the learner’s problem.
- A low score means the plan may be loosely relevant, but does not clearly help the learner achieve the intended learning outcome.

3. Background Adaptation (0-1): 
Does the plan adapt its explanations, examples, and interaction style to the learner’s background and likely learning needs?
- A high score means the learner’s background and likely seniority meaningfully shape how the content is taught.
- A low score means the plan is generic and could apply to many different learners with little change.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
Return ONLY a single JSON object with the final score and a detailed justification.

{
  "personalization_score": <integer from 0 to 10>,
  "justification": "<Your detailed analysis covering all three criteria, explaining the
  reasoning behind your score.>"
}
